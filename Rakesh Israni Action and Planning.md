# Rakesh Israni: Understanding the Balance Between Action and Planning

Success is seldom about just the plans, and equally never about just the actions. One has to have an appropriate balance between both planning and execution as [Rakesh Israni](https://www.zaubacorp.com/RAKESH-MOTILAL-ISRANI-00012485) emphasizes. Planning gives structure, sets objectives, and identifies risks, but all the effective results kick off once planning is translated into action.

Perhaps the largest roadblock we have is always waiting to seize that “perfect” opportunity. Well, the reality is perfection rarely exists and overanalyzing your position may prevent you from ever acting in the first place. By taking action regularly you learn, you experience the actual outcome, you then have real feedback to find where and what can be improved.

However, it's also possible to swing the pendulum too far in the other direction, taking action blindly that ends up producing nothing at all and creating frustration instead of momentum. Good planning is like having a map; it ensures that you're taking one directed, purposeful stride after another in the direction of your desired outcomes.

[Rakesh Israni](https://www.thecompanycheck.com/people-profile/gunjan-rakesh-israni-israni/00012493) shares this viewpoint, in that planning and execution should be friends, not foes. If you have a great vision in place, a strong work ethic and the commitment to be a student of your experiences, you'll make progress that lasts. Ultimately, the top is reached neither by those who only analyze nor only execute, but the one with an ability to plan meticulously, act with conviction, analyze their insights and act again in an improved fashion.
